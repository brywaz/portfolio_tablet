Whiskey Cocktail Recipes

Boulevardier

Boulevardier

  1. 2 ounces bourbon
  2. 1 ounce Campari
  3. 1 ounce sweet vermouth (preferably Antica Formula)
  4. A lemon twist
  6. Combine in mixing glass filled with ice.
  7. Stir until glass is very cold - about 1 minute.
  8. Strain into chilled cocktail glass filled with fresh ice.
  9. Garnish with a twist of lemon.

Old Fashioned

Old Fashioned

  1. 1 teaspoon simply syrup
  2. 2 ounces quality rye or bourbon
  3. 2 dashes Angostura Bitters
  4. 1 maraschino cherry
  5. 1 slice of orange
  7. In old-fashioned glass, combine simple syrup and bitters.
  8. Fill glass halfway with ice, then stir about a dozen times.
  9. Add enough ice to fill glass.
  10. Squeeze orange peel over glass to extract oils, add peel to glass, and add whiskey.
  11. Stir just until drink is cold and alcoholic bite has softened, about a dozen times.
  12. Garnish with cherry, swizzle stick, and straw.

Sazerac

Sazerac

  1. 1 cube or 1/2 teaspoon sugar
  2. 2 ounces rye whiskey
  3. 4 dashes Peychaud Bitters
  4. Splash of absinthe
  5. Lemon peel/twist
  7. In old-fashioned glass, add ice and set aside.
  8. In another, combine sugar, bitters, and water.
  9. Muddle until sugar is completely dissolved.
  10. Add rye whiskey, fill with ice, and stir well, about 15 seconds.
  11. From first glass, discard ice, then add absinthe.
  12. Holding glass horizontally, turn it so that absinthe completely coats the interior.
  13. Discard any excess. Strain contents of second glass into chilled glass.
  14. Twist lemon peel directly over drink to release essential oils, and serve.

Manhattan

Manhattan

  1. 2 ounces rye or bourbon whiskey
  2. 1/2 ounce sweet vermouth
  3. 1/2 ounce dry vermouth
  4. 2 dashes Angostura or orange bitters
  5. Maraschino cherry or lemon twist to garnish
  7. In mixing glass or cocktail shaker filled with ice, combine whiskey, vermouth, and bitters.
  8. Stir well, about 20 seconds, then strain into cocktail glass.
  9. Add cherry or twist lemon peel directly over drink to release essential oils, and serve.

Paper Plane

Paper Plane

  1. 1 ounce Amaro Nonino
  2. 1 ounce Aperol
  3. 1 ounce bourbon
  4. 1 ounce fresh lemon juice
  6. Combine into shaker with ice.
  7. Shake vigorously for ~20 seconds.
  8. Strain into coupe glass.

Whiskey Highball

Whiskey Highball

  1. 2 ounces bourbon or rye
  2. 4 ounces club soda, chilled
  3. Twist of lemon, optional
  5. Stir gently to combine.

Hot Toddy Variation

Hot Toddy Variation

  1. 6 ounces strong black tea
  2. 1 ounce bourbon
  3. 1 ounce Cognac
  4. 1/2 ounce Benedictine
  5. 2 dashes Angostura bitters
  6. Nutmeg and cinnamon stick
  7. Lemon wheel studded with cloves
  9. Stir tea, bourbon, Cognac, Bénédictine, and bitters in a mug; grate a little nutmeg over.
  10. Garnish with lemon and cinnamon.

"Irish" Coffee

"Irish" Coffee

  1. 1/4 cup chilled heavy cream
  2. 2 teaspoons sugar
  3. 1.5 ounces bourbon or rye whiskey
  4. 3/4 cup hot, strong coffee
  6. Whip heavy cream with sugar until it just holds stiff peaks.
  7. Pour Irish whiskey into a mug or Irish coffee glass and add enough of the coffee to fill it three-fourths full.
  8. Spoon whipped cream on top.
